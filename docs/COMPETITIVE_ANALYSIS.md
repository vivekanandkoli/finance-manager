## 🎯 Detailed Comparison

### 1. Mint (Intuit)
**Type:** Global leader, US-focused
**Strengths:**
- Automatic bank sync
- Advanced categorization
- Bill payment integration
- Large user base

**Weaknesses:**
- No Indian schemes (PPF, EPF, NPS)
- Privacy concerns (sells data to advertisers)
- Requires bank credentials
- Limited offline functionality
- US-centric features

**Our Advantage:**
- ✅ Indian schemes support (PPF, EPF, NPS, FD)
- ✅ Complete privacy (offline, local storage)
- ✅ No bank credentials required
- ✅ Multi-currency for NRI users
- ✅ Better income tracking

## ⚖️ Honest Disadvantages (vs Competitors)

### What We DON'T Have (Yet):

1. **Automatic Bank Sync**
   - Competitors: Mint, Personal Capital, ET Money
   - Impact: Requires manual entry
   - Mitigation: Import from CSV, faster manual entry UI

2. **AI-Powered Categorization**
   - Competitors: Mint, Walnut
   - Impact: Manual categorization needed
   - Mitigation: Smart suggestions based on history

3. **Receipt Scanning**
   - Competitors: Mint, Expensify
   - Impact: Can't digitize physical receipts
   - Mitigation: Take photos, add descriptions

4. **Real-Time Investment Tracking**
   - Competitors: Personal Capital, ET Money
   - Impact: Manual NAV updates
   - Mitigation: Quarterly update reminders

5. **Credit Score Tracking**
   - Competitors: Money View, CIBIL
   - Impact: No credit score visibility
   - Mitigation: Focus on credit utilization tracking

6. **Mobile App**
   - Competitors: All of them
   - Impact: Web-only for now
   - Mitigation: Responsive web design, PWA possible
